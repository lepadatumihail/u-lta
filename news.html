U-LTA News: Announcing the International Conference on Electric Airships - Decarbonizing Aviation


We are excited to announce the International Conference on Electric Airships - Decarbonizing Aviation, a groundbreaking event that aims to explore the potential of electric airships in revolutionizing aviation while reducing carbon emissions. This conference is hosted by University of Manitoba, University of Erlangen-Nuremberg, IMIEU and the Fraunhofer-Institut and will bring together leading experts, researchers, and industry professionals from around the world to discuss and exchange ideas on various aspects of electric airships.

The conference will cover a wide range of topics, including technical aspects, applications, and environmental considerations. Participants will have the opportunity to delve into discussions about the construction and design of airships, the construction of low and high-altitude drones, sustainable aviation by airships, power electronics and batteries for airships, solar energy applications for airships, and much more.

We invite researchers, scientists, engineers, and professionals from related fields to submit their abstracts for presentation at the conference. The timeline for abstract submission is from May 1st to July 9th, 2023. This deadline is very soon, please send your abstract! Abstracts should be sent via email to christoph.pflaum@fau.de. The program committee will review the submissions, and decisions will be published on July 15th, 2023.

Registration for the conference is now open, and we encourage early registration to secure your spot. Early Bird registration is closing soon on July 31st, 2023. The late registration period will end on September 1st, 2023.

By participating in this conference, attendees will gain valuable insights into the future of electric airships, discover innovative technologies, and explore the potential of hydrogen as an energy source and lifting gas for airships. Additionally, topics such as certification, regulation, and integration into existing aerospace, as well as infrastructure and ground handling of airships, will also be discussed.

Join us at the International Conference on Electric Airships - Decarbonizing Aviation and be part of the cutting-edge advancements that are shaping the future of aviation. Mark your calendars for this exciting conference, and together, let's create a greener and more sustainable aviation industry.

We look forward to your active participation in this transformative event.

Carribean Islands Meeting


We were honoured to provide input on Lighter Than Air Technologies / Airship solutions for the Small Island Developing States in the Caribbean during the "First Extraordinary Meeting of the Special Committee on the Development of Trade and External Economic Relations and the Special Committee on Transport" organised by the Association of Caribbean States and the ECLAC. Thank you, Dr. Ricardo Sanchez, for inviting IMIEU and the U-LTA platform to take part in this special event and thank you, Kay Sealy, for chairing it!

The event demonstrated that LTA Technology has excellent functionalities to facilitate resilient economic development by improving environmental surveillance, sustainable telecommunications, low carbon cargo transport and more environmentally friendly aviation for recreation and tourism, and aid in case of natural calamities. The insights given were based on a study conducted by ECLAC and it was interesting to see the perspective of the World Food Programme on the potential usage of airship technology. Excellent presentations were given amongst others by Bob Fowler from Aerovehicles Inc., Prof. Barry Prentice from Buoyant Aircraft Systems International and the University of Manitoba, Octave Jolimoy from Flying Whales, Igor Pasternak from Aeroscraft, and Mark Dorey from Straightline Aviation Ltd.

European Green Week 2021



During the European Green Week an informative webinar will be hosted on the topic of Lighter Than Air (LTA) aviation. The goal is to inform policy makers, industry parties, researchers, and the public at large about the benefits of LTA technology, also to attain the objectives of the New European Green Deal, the EU Flight Path 2050 strategy, and the new more ambitious 55 % objective regarding the implementation of renewable energy. In fact, the technology is a key driver to decarbonise aviation activities, such as those related to cargo, accessing remote regions, carrying out urban surveillance, for example, replacing or complementing helicopters. The technology can be also be applied in emergency aid situations, which could appear because of the physical risks of climate change. In order to achieve the goals set out in the Paris Agreement and deliver on the EU Green Deal, the technology can greatly reduce the environmental footprint of aviation activities.

LTA is gaining attention at the European and global level and currently is the ambition of the sector is to upscale the use of this technology. Therefore, the ambitions of the U-LTA platform, initiated and coordinated by IMIEU in 2016 is to 1. further enhance the visibility of LTA technology with European and International institutions and investors 2. mobilize financing and investments in order to solve challenges regarding the upscaling of the necessary ground and related operation and maintenance infrastructure 3. Improve the certification procedures, the safety of ground operations, and the efficiency of maintenance operations, in addition to promote certified training of staff. To date, the opportunities for this technology are present and during the partner event taking place at June 3rd 2021 during the EU Green Week, some of the partners of the U-LTA platform will proudly showcase their applications and commercialisation of LTA technology.

Organised by IMIEU (The Institute for Infrastructure, Environment and Innovation) and the Quebec Government Office in Brussels.
